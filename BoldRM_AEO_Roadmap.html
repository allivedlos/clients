What is AEO?

Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) is the intentional practice of making your business the answer that AI-powered tools — ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Mode — deliver when users ask relevant questions. Unlike SEO (ranking on page 1), AEO is about being the answer itself, not just a result to click.

The book distinguishes two types of AI engines: Pre-trained engines (Claude, Llama — work from frozen knowledge) and real-time engines (Perplexity, ChatGPT with browsing, Google AI Mode — actively crawl the web). boldRM needs to be optimized for both, but real-time engines are the primary opportunity.

Research finding from The AEO Blueprint: Of 7,923 URLs analyzed across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Mode — 57.8% came from URLs ranking on Google/Bing, but 42.2% came from sources not ranking on traditional search at all. AI doesn't just scrape the top 3 results. Brand websites and blogs are the #1 and #2 most-cited source types across all three engines.

The BRAIN Framework

The strategy that drives every phase of this roadmap, as defined in The AEO Blueprint:

B Brand Representation Consistent NAP + brand presence across all platforms AI trusts (directories, communities, media). Inconsistency = AI won't cite you.
R Recognition / Authority Deep, original, experience-based content that only boldRM could produce. No AI-recycled articles — that's the "death spiral." Unlinked brand mentions now count as authority signals.
A Authority & Citations Be cited on external platforms — agent blogs, Inman, RESA, Houzz, Reddit. AI triangulates trust from Social Proof + Structured Fact + First-Party Truth (your site).
I Indexability JSON-LD schema (served server-side, never via JavaScript), llms.txt, clean URLs, sitemap, entity linking. AI bots can't execute JS — schema injected via GTM is invisible to them.
N Net New Content Every piece of content must add genuinely new information. The "death spiral": AI writes articles → AI cites those → content gene pool shrinks → LLMs stop treating it as authoritative.

PHASE 1 Fix & Index — Critical Technical Foundation Week 1–2 · Do these first

The book is clear: schema markup is "the single most important and unambiguous factor" for AI visibility. But there's a critical technical trap — AI bots (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot) cannot execute JavaScript. If your schema is injected via GTM or client-side JS, AI crawlers are blind to it. All schema must be in the raw HTML served from the server. Fix these issues before anything else.

Critical finding from The AEO Blueprint: "Platforms like GPTBot, ClaudeBot, and PerplexityBot do not execute JavaScript. That means any JSON-LD added after the page loads via GTM is invisible to AI crawlers." Webflow renders server-side by default — this is an advantage. Do NOT add schema via Google Tag Manager. Add it directly in Webflow's Page Settings → Custom Code.

PHASE 2 Brand Representation — NAP + Entity Consistency Everywhere Week 2–4 · The BRAIN "B" layer

The book introduces the concept of "Brand Density" — how present, visible, and influential your brand is across the digital landscape. It's not about showing up anywhere; it's about showing up in the specific places AI engines crawl when building consensus. The book identifies three pillars: Reach (are you there?), Recognition (are you contributing?), and Reputation (reviews and citations).

The "Answer Budget" Framework (from the book's research): AI builds answers in two stages. Stage 1: It scans Reddit, directories, and community platforms to build a "consideration set" — a list of trusted brands. Stage 2: It then visits those brands' websites directly for specific facts. boldRM must appear in Stage 1 sources (Yelp, Houzz, agent forums) AND have clean schema on the site for Stage 2.

PHASE 3 Deep Authority — Original Content + Topical Depth Week 4–8 · The BRAIN "R" + "A" layer

The book introduces the concept of Topical Authority — AI rewards brands that go deep, not wide. Don't try to be a generalist; own the "home staging in the Bay Area" niche completely. The book's research found that pages cited by AI average 2,288–3,400 words depending on the engine — depth matters. But the #1 rule is: every article must be net-new.

The AI death spiral (The AEO Blueprint, Ch.9): Using ChatGPT to write staging articles → those get indexed → AI engines cite them when writing new articles → the content gene pool shrinks → LLMs stop treating it as authoritative. The book states: "LLMs just quote themselves and start to recognize the pattern of: 'Well, this is not net new information.'" Every boldRM article must use real data: actual addresses, real sale prices, genuine before/after outcomes.
